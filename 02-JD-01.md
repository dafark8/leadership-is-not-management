# Job Description - Sample 1 - UX Practice Lead

## Position Summary

The User Experience Practice Lead is a trusted partner with key business stakeholders and User Experience team members. They advocate for Human Centered Design practices internally, with external partners, and client stakeholders. The UX Lead manages a team of designers and researchers and oversees large-scale projects. 

## Skills Summary

The UX Lead fosters best practices and advocates for HCD. UX Leads have an expertise in at least one area of practice with a strong familiarity in 2 additional areas. UX areas of practice may include but are not limited to content strategy, user research (qualitative or quantitative), information architecture, user experience, interaction design, visual design. 

## Key Objectives

### Objective 1 – Technical Leader

The UX Lead is a leader in at least one UX discipline and supports project delivery. 

- Generates product strategies and timelines
-Leverages existing design and research methods while adapting, adopting, and creating new methods as appropriate
- Communicates through storytelling, written and verbal channels the pros and cons of options backed by research, best practices, and input provided across the project team. 
- Delivers information based on the dynamic needs of the clients including workshops, wireframes, sitemaps, research protocols, and findings. 
- Evangelizes how to incorporate UX practices into an agile development lifecycle 
- Collaborates with other discipline leads on project delivery and execution

### Objective 2 – UX Culture & Process

The UX Design lead advocates for UX practices within the organization

- Actively shares knowledge within the UX team and across the broader organization 
- Identifies opportunities to codify and standardize practices for the UX team 
- Empowers non-UX team member to contribute to research and design activities
- Supports team members with suggestions on approaches and solutions to changing timelines and project needs
- Mentors team members and establishes a community of open collaboration and continued improvement 
- Contributes to staffing decisions, resourcing, and partners with Talent Acquisition
- Represents the organization through blog posts and community events (speaking and attending)
- Refines and contributes to the HCD and UX delivery playbook including templates, operating procedures, and approach

### Objective 3 – Empower the Individual 

The UX Design Lead coordinates with leadership to support individual goals
- Coordinates with team to identify individual and professional S.M.A.R.T goals 
- Ensures the team balances work and time-off, supporting personal and professional commitments
- Champions change and recognize team and individual achievements
- Mediates disagreements and challenges within the team 

### Objective 4 – Business Development

The UX Design Lead supports business development “solutioning”

- Supports with tech challenges/demos from a HCD/UX perspective
- Aligns with internal stakeholders on positioning and approach of new business
- Ideates approaches to address client needs
- Contributes to resourcing decisions
