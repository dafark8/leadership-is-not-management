# Leadership != Management

Welcome to *Leadership != Management*, a look into my views on leading, mentoring, and managing individuals and teams. By way of quick introduction, I am a UX Leader with a focus on research, strategy, and collaboration. Curious why a leader is writing about management? Read on! 

**What’s in this for you?** Having grown from confused leader to confident manager, I aim to share my toolkit as one resource others may pull from. 

**What’s in this for me?** I first shared this content in the winter of 2023-2024 when I was impacted in the wave of tech-layoffs. I am now moving this content from a google doc to a github repo to: 
1. Expand my skills and refresh my comfort with markdown 
1. Gain confidence with github and terminal commans
1. Share my perspectives to engage the community 
1. By setting this repo to public, invite perspectives via PRs. *This is a hypothesis and I will review PRs as they come and see how this experiment works out*. 

**Who am I?** I have been mentoring individuals formally and informally since 2012. This includes conference and UX Community (shout out to [PhillyCHI](https://phillychi.org/)) friendships and formal mentoring through places I have worked. I had my first formal managing responsibility in 2015. Like many new managers, I didn’t understand the differences between mentoring, leading, and management. I knew more about management via positive and negative experiences. My idea was “management by aversion”. By that, I managed by doing the opposite of what I considered to be poor management experiences. 

Since then, I have taken training, read books, articles, and hot takes on the theory, approach, and techniques to managing teams and individuals. I’ve established a series of tools and techniques, distilling those into quick checklists and activities which I’ll share with you. 

My first structured draft was written in 2020, edited as I moved from leading a small UX Research team to a more general UX Manager role in 2022. I’ve revised this as I shifted from managing designers to managing a pool of UX Researchers (2023) and back to a team of mixed backgrounds (2024) and a dedicated research team (2025). That is to say, this is a living document. 

I’m writing this first for myself. To hold myself accountable. Think of this as my Delta File (more on that in Chapter 1). If you find value in the writing below, awesome. Let’s chat. If not, I want to hear how you think differently about this content too. 

For now, welcome. 

I’ll be adding content to this document and posting about the release.
